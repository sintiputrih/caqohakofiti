Who is Londa Butler?

Londa Butler is a name that many fans of American professional basketball player Jimmy Butler may recognize. The mother of the Miami Heat guard, she, rose to popularity after news of her harsh treatment towards her son started doing the rounds.

Londa Butler kicked her son Jimmy Butler out of her home when he was 13. Moreover, she was also against Jimmy’s dream of becoming a professional basketball player.

Quick facts

Londa Butler childhood

Londa Butler was born on March 10, 1958, in Tomball, Texas, USA. Currently, she is 65 years old. 

Londa raised her kids as a single mother. Her husband’s name is not known to anyone. He must have left her when her children were very small.

Londa Butler seems to be around 5 ft 6 inches tall. She has dark brown eyes and black-colored hair. She usually styles her hair into cornrows.

Londa Butler is a follower of Christianity. She is an American. Likewise, Londa belongs to African-American ethnic background.

About Londa Butler’s son, Jimmy Butler

NBA player Jimmy Butler truly has a rags-to-riches story. His childhood was filled with many hardships and familial struggles. 

Jimmy never got to know his father. His father abandoned Jimmy and his mother when he was just an infant. 

Moreover, his family also faced many financial hardships, making it difficult for them to meet. Likewise, Jimmy’s mother, Londa, was not warm and caring. 

When Jimmy was only 13, his mother kicked him out of their house. Recalling his mother’s harsh treatment of him, Jimmy Butler remembered his mother’s final words to him in a 2011 interview. According to Butler, his mother told him, “I don’t like the look of you. You gotta go.” 

As a result of his mother’s maltreatment, Jimmy moved out of his family’s home in the Houston suburb of Tomball. Following this, he bounced between his friends’ homes and lived there for a few weeks, then moved into his other friend’s house.

Jimmy’s life changed forever when he met Jordan Leslie in a summer basketball league before his senior year at Tomball High School. Jordan Leslie, a freshman football and basketball player at Tomball High School, challenged Jimmy to a three-point shooting contest. 

Jimmy and Jordan instantly hit it off. They became close friends, and Jordan even let Jimmy stay at his house. 

Jordan’s mother and stepfather initially were reluctant to let Jimmy stay with them because they were already struggling to raise six other children. However, after Jordan’s repeated insistence, they finally welcomed Jimmy into their household after a few months.

Jordan Leslie’s mother, Michelle Lambert, fulfilled a maternal figure that Jimmy Butler lacked in his life.

Unlike Jimmy’s biological mother, Londa Butler, who never supported Jimmy’s dreams of pursuing a basketball career, Michelle Lambert always encouraged Jimmy to follow his dreams. She adopted Jimmy, and as his mother, she always pushed Jimmy to work hard in school and try to improve his basketball skills as much as possible. 

Michelle continued being a part of Jimmy’s life after high school graduation. When he went off to play college basketball at Marquette University, Michelle provided Jimmy with the moral support he needed. 

This was the first time that Jimmy had stayed outside of Texas. Due to this, he felt pretty homesick and decided to drop out of Marquette University to move back to Texas. 

Nevertheless, Michelle reminded Jimmy of what an incredible once-in-a-lifetime opportunity he was leaving behind. She advised him to remain at Marquette and told him not to quit so quickly on his dreams. 

Michelle’s advice changed Jimmy’s life forever. After that, Jimmy Butler’s collegiate basketball career was phenomenal.

Jimmy Butler was declared for the NBA draft soon after his Junior year due to his exceptional performance. Jimmy ended up being the last pick in the first round of the 2011 NBA draft by the Chicago Bulls. He was the 30th overall pick by the Chicago Bulls. 

Following this, Jimmy Butler ended up having an illustrious basketball career.  He is a five-time NBA All-Star, a four-time All-NBA Team honoree, and a five-time NBA All-Defensive Team honoree.

Jimmy Butler’s Relationship with Londa Butler

Despite having a troubled relationship with his biological parents in the past, it seems that Jimmy Butler and his parents have reconciled. However, in an interview, Butler hinted that he maintains a close relationship with his parents. 

He said,

“I don’t hold grudges. I still talk to my family. My mom. My father. We love each other. That’s never going to change.”

Furthermore, there were reports that Jimmy’s biological mother, Londa Butler, reached out to Jimmy after signing an NBA Deal for over $30 million.

Despite his troubled childhood and strained familial relationships, Jimmy has always maintained that he dislikes dwelling on the past and hates it when people treat him like a charity case.

Michael Jordan and Londa Butler

Since no information was available regarding the whereabouts of Jimmy Butler’s father, rumors started swirling on the internet, suggesting that Michael Jordan was Jimmy Butler’s father. The rumors result from the conjectures claiming that Michael Jordan abandoned a child in 1988. 

Jordan was allegedly married in 1988, but he separated from his wife after the birth of their child. Similarly, the rumors also say that Michael Jordan’s former wife kicked their child out of the house at 13 due to financial hardships. 

Since these rumors align with Jimmy Butler’s tragic childhood story, people started speculating that the five-time NBA All-Star player was the long-lost son of legendary player Michael Jordan. Moreover, their uncanny resemblance also further solidified the rumors. 

Nevertheless, Jimmy Butler is not Michael Jordan’s son. This is because Michael Jordan is said to have abandoned his child in 1988. This was a year before Jimmy Butler’s birth.

Furthermore, no solid grounds link Michael Jordan and Jimmy Butler’s biological mother, Londa Butler. Hence, despite the longstanding rumors regarding Jimmy Butler’s relation to Michael Jordan, they are not related.
