Mohamed Sanu is a professional Football player. Mohamed Sanu plays for the San Francisco 49ers of the National Football League. Similarly, the athlete has garnered many fans and followers over the years.

Early Life

Mohamed Sanu was born in the year 1989 and celebrates his birthday on the 22 of August every year. He was born to a Muslim family in Sayreville, New Brunswick, New Jersey, USA. Similarly, he is 34 years old as of now. Mohamed belongs to the American nationality and was born under the star sign Leo.

Moving towards his parents, his father is Samuel Sanu who happens to be a former professional football player. His mother Aminata Koroma is a migrant who moved to the US from Sierra Leone. Likewise, he has a sister and brother, whose names have not been revealed yet.

While growing up Mohamed has been very athletic and loved to play any kind of sport. Similarly, as his father was a professional football player he was drawn to the sport naturally. Moreover, his father helped him train and excel in sports.

Mohamed Sanu – Education

Mohamed Sanu has played football both for his high school and University. However, the details regarding his early education and schooling are not available. For his high school, he joined South Brunswick High School which is located in his hometown.

Similarly, he played as a quarterback for his high school team and has helped the team win on several occasions. After he graduated from high school, he went on to join Rutgers University in the year 2009. Likewise, it was during his university days when he started playing football as a true Freshman.

Career and Professional Life

Mohamed Sanu had played football for his high school and University. He made his debut in professional football with the team Cincinnati Bengals. The young player was drafted in the 2012 NFL draft and signed in a four-year deal for USD 2.7 million. Similarly, he played his first professional football match on September 23, 2012, against the Washington Redskins. Mohamed also became the first Bengal wide receiver to throw a touchdown pass before an NFL reception pass.
