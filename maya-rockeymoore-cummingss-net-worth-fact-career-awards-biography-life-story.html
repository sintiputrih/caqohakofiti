Maya Rockeymoore Cummings is an American consultant, politician, and former Chairwoman of the Maryland Democratic Party. Cummings previously ran for the Governor of Maryland and even owns a consulting firm at Washington D.C. Maya is popularly known to be the widow of a late popular politician, Elijah Cummings. She resigned her post as chair of the Maryland Democratic Party to run for her late husband’s congressional seat in the 2020 Maryland’s 7th congressional district special election.

Maya Rockeymoore Cummings Career Highlights

  • Maya Rockeymoore Cummings started her professional career after she became an Administrative Assistant to the Director at the Marion County Department of Health in Indiana in 1995.
  • She later became a legislative fellow for the Congressional Black Caucus under former Rep. Melvin Watt. in September 1997.
  • In 1998, she worked as a professional staffer at the Ways and Means Social Security Subcommittee.
  • In January 1999, she began working as the Chief of Staff for former Rep. Charles Rangel.
  • In 2001, Cummings left Congress to become Senior Resident Scholar for Health and Income Security for the National Urban League.
  • Two years later, in 2003, she joined the Congressional Black Caucus Foundation as Vice President of Research and Programs.
  • In 2005, she started a D.C.-based policy consulting firm, “Global Policy Solutions”.
  • Maya was named an Aspen Institute Henry Crown Fellow in 2004 and received Running Start’s 2007 Young Women to Watch Award.
  • She previously served as the Director of Leadership for Healthy Communities, a national program office of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation.
  • In 2012, Maya started the 501c3 non-profit tank, the Center for Global Policy Solutions.
  • In 2017, she ran for the Governor of Maryland in the 2018 election. However, after husband, Elijah Cummings was hospitalized she dropped out of the race.
  • In December 2018, Rockeymoore Cummings was elected as chairwoman of the Maryland Democratic Party.
  • On November 11, 2019, Cummings announced that she will run for her late husband’s congressional seat in the “2020 Maryland’s 7th Congressional District Special Election”.
  • Further, Cummings is the co-author of the Action Strategies for Healthy Communities Toolkit.
