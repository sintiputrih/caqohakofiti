Career

Alvarez established himself as a good boxer from the start of his career, playing and winning contests one after the other.

In 2007, he won his first professional championship when he defeated Mexican boxer Ricardo Cano to earn the ‘Jalisco’ welterweight title.

Similarly, he defeated Mexican boxer Gabriel Martinez to win the ‘WBA Fedecentro’ welterweight championship on April 18, 2008.

On January 17, 2009, Alvarez won the ‘NABF’ and ‘WBO Latino’ welterweight belts, continuing his winning streak.

In 2013, he suffered his first professional defeat to American boxer Floyd Mayweather Jr.

The loss, however, could not mar the fighting spirit of Álvarez, who went on to win three bouts against three different boxers in 2015.

Then, after defeating Miguel Cotto to capture the WBC, The Ring, and lineal middleweight crowns, he faced Amir Khan for title defense on May 7, 2016.

Furthermore, he defeated Khan by knocking him out in the sixth round.

Later in his career, he fought Liam Smith to capture the WBO light-middleweight championship. Canelo then went on to win back-to-back fights against Golovkin.

Canelo Alvarez has a professional boxing record of 53 wins, one defeat, and two draws in 2021.

In addition, he has won 37 of his 38 fights via knockout.
